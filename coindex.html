Exhibit A For Satan or For God?

Posted January 30, 2018

Imagine a courtroom scene, the greatest one that could or will ever be. Our Almighty God and Father, the Most High, sits behind the bench. On one side, arguing, is Satan. On the other side, defending, is our Lord and Savior Jesus, the Christ. All around in the whole host of heaven and every person who has ever lived on earth.

No one is there to report or to entertain themselves. Everyone waits with bated breath.

"I call my witness," you might imagine Satan saying. "Exhibit A-- [YOUR NAME HERE]."

Imagine that Satan has called you as a witness. There will be no need for you to say or do anything. In that day, all would have been said and done completely. It is your life that would serve as evidence, and if Satan called you to help his case ... That would be a sorrowful thing.

The account of Job proves a few things. First, there are way more things going on in life for mankind to account for, or ever to know about. Lots of things happen that we can't explain becasue we don't the first bit about it. This is why our only security is in the

Lord. Second, Satan is an accuser. "Does Job fear God for nothing?" Satan asked in the first chapter of Job. He might has well have asked, "Could any of your creation truly love you?"

Keep in mind that this accusation is against God, not Job. Job's life would either be a testimony to God's glory and worthiness and holiness, or evidence to support Satan's attempt to accuse God to the contrary. Well, Job was faithful. Are we?

Our lives will be a testimony for one side. Which one will it be?
